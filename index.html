Trust & safety

Medical review & content governance

Patient-facing health content carries real responsibility. Here’s how this tool is meant to be kept accurate, safe, and honest about its limits.

Draft content — awaiting sign-off by the collaborating knee specialist

How content is approved

  • Every label, description, symptom and “when to seek advice” note is drafted here, then reviewed and signed off by a qualified knee specialist before it’s presented as accurate.
  • Each structure carries a review date and reviewer initials in the content file (see the developer notes).
  • Urgent “warning sign” wording is reviewed with particular care, since getting it wrong could cause real harm.
  • Content is re-reviewed on a fixed schedule and whenever guidelines change.

Our safety boundaries

  • This tool never diagnoses. It uses careful language like “may be associated with”.
  • It cannot replace a physical examination, scan, or professional judgement.
  • The symptom guide runs fully on your device. Nothing is stored or transmitted.
  • Prominent warnings flag symptoms that need prompt in-person care.

Know Your Knee — an educational prototype. Not medical advice, and not a diagnostic tool. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional about your own knee. In an emergency, contact your local emergency number.
